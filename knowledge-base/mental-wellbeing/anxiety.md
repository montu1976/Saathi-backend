# Anxiety

## Overview

Anxiety here means everyday worry, tension, racing thoughts, physical jitters, and avoidance that interfere with life—not a clinical diagnosis. In India, people often call it "tension," "overthinking," or "weakness." Academic pressure, marriage timelines, job insecurity, family conflict, and health fears are common triggers. This guide helps users ground themselves, seek appropriate support, and make small practical changes without labelling them or promising cures.

## Root causes

- Uncertainty without actionable plan
- Sleep deprivation amplifying threat perception
- Caffeine, skipped meals, irregular routine
- Perfectionism and fear of disappointing family
- Past trauma or bullying resurfacing under stress
- Financial instability or job insecurity
- Relationship conflict without resolution
- Social comparison on Instagram/LinkedIn
- Lack of trusted person to verbalise fears
- Major life transition without adjustment time

## Emotions usually involved

- Fear and dread
- Irritability
- Restlessness
- Shame about "being weak"
- Sadness underneath worry
- Frustration at body reactions
- Loneliness despite being around people
- Hyperalertness
- Relief after avoidance—reinforcing cycle
- Hope when one calm hour appears

## Warning signs

- Panic attacks frequent and disabling
- Unable to leave home for weeks
- Self-harm or suicidal thoughts
- Substance reliance to calm down daily
- Weight loss or gain rapid from stress
- Work or studies failing for months
- Chest pain unchecked—see doctor to rule physical
- Relationships collapsing from constant reassurance seeking
- Sleep under 4 hours sustained
- Physical tremors interfering with tasks

## Healthy principles

- Anxiety is a signal—not always enemy; assess real vs imagined threat
- Small exposures beat total avoidance long-term
- Sleep, food, movement are baseline medicine
- Breathing slows body before mind argues
- Limit news/WhatsApp when spiralling
- Professional help is strength in high-functioning India too
- Name feeling to tame it—"this is anxiety wave"
- One problem one timeframe—don't solve entire life at 2am
- Family may not understand—find one safe outlet
- Progress is jagged graph not straight line

## Practical first steps

- 4-7-8 breathing 3 rounds now
- Write top 3 worries—circle one actionable today
- 10-minute walk without phone
- Reduce caffeine after 2pm for 7 days
- Tell one person "I'm tense lately"—no full story required
- Box breathing before exam/interview
- Schedule 15-min "worry time" later—not 2am
- Grounding: 5 things see, 4 touch, 3 hear
- One task start 5 min rule—momentum helps
- Tele-counsellor or campus counsellor one session

## Long-term strategies

- CBT-skills apps or therapist for thought patterns
- Regular sleep window same time
- Exercise 4x week moderate
- Financial buffer reduces background hum
- Boundary with relatives who trigger worry
- Journaling patterns—triggers and wins
- Gradual exposure to avoided situations with support
- Meditation/mindfulness 10 min daily habit
- Medication only via doctor—no shame if needed
- Build identity beyond one stress domain (only exams, only job)

## Things to avoid

- Googling symptoms at 1am
- Reassurance loops texting ex or parent hourly
- Alcohol every night to sleep
- Quitting job/school impulsively to escape anxiety
- Labeling self "pagal" to family
- Toxic positivity—"just chill"
- Overcommitting to fix anxiety in one day
- Isolating completely
- Caffeine stacking energy drinks in prep
- Comparing to influencer "calm" posts

## Questions AI should ask before advising

1. What triggered this week specifically?
2. Body symptoms—heart, stomach, breath?
3. Sleep hours and caffeine?
4. Avoiding something important?
5. How long pattern—days or months?
6. Panic attacks or constant hum?
7. Family knows or stigma barrier?
8. Exam/job/relationship which domain?
9. Self-harm thoughts ever?
10. Tried breathing/walk yet today?
11. Doctor checked physical causes?
12. What would 10% relief look like today?

## Indian cultural considerations

- "Tension mat le" dismissal common—validate anyway
- Prayer and faith can soothe—integrate if user values
- Yoga pranayama familiar entry point
- Mental health stigma—frame as performance/sleep help if needed
- Joint family—less privacy to decompress
- Women expected to absorb stress silently—permission to speak
- Men discouraged from vulnerability—normalize
- Coaching/hostel anxiety epidemic—structural not personal flaw
- Marriage market anxiety on appearance/age—societal not individual
- Cost of therapy—tele options and college counsellors

## Different advice depending on age

### Teenager

- Board/exam anxiety—parents loop makes worse
- Safe adult or counsellor if panic at school
- Phone limits at night

### College student

- Placement + relationship + family—triple load
- Hostel isolation—peer or counsellor

### Working professional

- Sunday scaries and toxic boss—boundary and job plan
- EMI + performance anxiety—financial clarity

### Married

- In-law or spouse conflict fuel
- Couple communication without blame

### Parent

- Child health worry—facts vs google spiral
- Model calm regulation for kids

### Senior citizen

- Health scan anxiety—doctor schedule not rumination
- Loneliness after retirement factor

## If children are involved

- Anxious parent—kids absorb; explain age-appropriately "mummy stressed busy work"
- Do not use kids for reassurance
- School pressure on child—separate your anxiety from theirs

## If finances are involved

- EMI worry—spreadsheet with dates reduces unknown
- Avoid impulsive investments from fear
- Job loss anxiety—runway calculation

## If mental health is involved

- This topic IS wellbeing—still not diagnosis
- Persistent panic, agoraphobia, OCD-like rituals, suicidal ideation need professional evaluation
- Helplines: Kiran 1800-599-0019, Vandrevala
- Medication decision doctor only
- Anxiety and depression overlap—watch sleep/mood duration

## Common mistakes people make

1. Fighting every thought intellectually at 2am
2. Avoiding all social contact
3. Seeking endless reassurance
4. No sleep routine
5. Caffeine increase during exams
6. Dr Google self-diagnosis
7. Shaming self for body symptoms
8. Expecting meditation to work day one perfectly
9. Telling entire family before one safe person
10. Ignoring weeks of dysfunction

## Recommended conversation style

- Normalize without minimising
- Body-first calming then problem-solving
- No clinical labels unless user used them
- Short tools not lecture
- Escalate to professional gently if severe
